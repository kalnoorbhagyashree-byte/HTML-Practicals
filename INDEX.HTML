Hyderabadi Veg Biryani (Famous)

A fragrant, layered dum-style biryani made with mixed vegetables, basmati rice and classic Hyderabadi spices.

Prep: 30 mins • Cook: 40 mins • Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked 30 minutes
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans, potato) — chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint, 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, 2 tsp biryani masala
  • 4–5 whole spices: bay leaf, 2 cardamom, 3 cloves, 1" cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)
  • 2 tbsp fried onions (for garnish)
  • 1–2 tbsp lemon juice

Method

  1. Parboil the soaked rice with salt and a few whole spices until 70% done. Drain and keep aside.
  2. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices, then sliced onions; fry until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; sauté until raw aroma disappears. Add chopped vegetables and cook 4–5 minutes.
  4. Stir in yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, biryani masala, salt, mint and cilantro. Cook until vegetables are just tender. Finish with lemon juice.
  5. Reduce heat. Layer half of the rice over the vegetable masala. Sprinkle half the saffron milk (if using) and some fried onions and herbs. Repeat with remaining rice and toppings.
  6. Seal the pot with a tight lid (use dough or foil if needed). Cook on very low heat (dum) for 20–25 minutes until rice is fully done and flavors meld.
  7. Let rest 10 minutes, gently fluff with a fork, serve hot with raita and salad.

Tips

  • Parboiling prevents mushy rice — test by biting a grain (should have a slight core).
  • Use fresh spices and long-grain basmati for authentic aroma and texture.
  • For richer flavor add a little melted butter or a few drops of rose/water kewra while layering.
